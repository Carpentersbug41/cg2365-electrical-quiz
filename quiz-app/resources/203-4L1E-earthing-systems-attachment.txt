Lesson: 203.4L1E - Earthing Systems (Noob): TT, TN-S, TN-C-S
Unit: 203
Audience: Level 2 electrical learner

Use the attachment one section at a time.
When teaching a turn, use only the current section and do not pull teaching points forward from later sections.

=== SECTION: Intro ===
- Name the three main earthing systems used in installations.
- Identify the core parts that make ADS work safely.
- Explain where the earth path comes from in TT, TN-S, and TN-C-S.
- Recognise an earthing system from a simple site description.
- Open the lesson naturally and explain that the session will run in short chunks followed by questions.

=== SECTION: Teach/Check 1 ===
Core idea
ADS keeps people safe by disconnecting the supply quickly when a fault makes exposed metalwork live.

Must teach
- ADS stands for Automatic Disconnection of Supply.
- An exposed conductive part is touchable metalwork that could become live in a fault.
- The protective conductor gives fault current a low-resistance path back to the source.
- The protective device trips when the fault current is high enough.
- The MET is the common point where earthing paths meet.

Concrete anchor
- Use a metal kettle or appliance casing to explain what happens if a live conductor touches metalwork.

Question targets
1. What does ADS stand for?
2. What is an exposed conductive part?
3. Which component trips to disconnect the supply during a fault?
Deeper question: Why does earthing stop you from becoming the path to earth during a fault?

=== SECTION: Teach/Check 2 ===
Core idea
The main difference between TT, TN-S, and TN-C-S is where the earth path comes from.

Must teach
- TT uses a local earth electrode driven into the ground.
- TN-S gets a separate supplier earth, often through the cable sheath.
- TN-C-S uses a combined PEN conductor in the supply which is split into earth and neutral at the intake.
- You identify the system by looking at the physical arrangement at the service head.

Why it matters
- You need the right system before you judge the earth path and how fault protection will operate.

Question targets
1. Name the three earthing systems being compared.
2. Which one uses a local earth electrode?
3. Which one uses a PEN conductor in the supply?
Deeper question: Why does the earthing arrangement matter when deciding whether a fault will disconnect safely?

=== SECTION: Guided practice 1 ===
Scenario
- You arrive at a remote cabin. There is no earth wire from the supplier cable. A green/yellow conductor leaves the MET and goes out to a copper rod in the ground.

Guided prompts
1. Does the earth path come from the supplier cable or from the ground?
2. What is the name of the rod or buried part that provides the earth path?
3. Based on that description, which earthing system is it?

Expected points
- from the ground
- earth electrode or earth rod
- TT

=== SECTION: Practice 1 ===
Independent checks
1. How many main earthing system types are covered here?
2. What does MET stand for?
3. Which system uses a PEN conductor?
4. What is the green/yellow fault-path conductor usually called?

=== SECTION: Integrative 1 ===
Prompt
- Explain how the physical earthing arrangement changes the fault path and why that matters for ADS.

Key points
- different arrangements provide different earth paths
- that changes loop impedance
- ADS depends on enough fault current to trip the protective device quickly
